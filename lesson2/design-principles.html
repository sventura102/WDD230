PARC:Proximity

thesmithcenter.com

The proximity on the Smith Center page immediately displays a theater banner that shows us why we're here: to look for theater tickets. There is already an invitation to the user, urging them to (in a sophisticated way) join them for a show.

PARC:Alignment

shepherdeye.com

Aside from the image that I provided, there are plenty of examples of alignment on this website. When you hover over the dropdown bars, plenty of information that the user would typically need is found. From the image that I provided, you can easily see that the alignment between the columns of the two blocks that show what the user may be looking for in a broader, easy-to-read way for people that probably can't see the dropdown bars.

PARC:Repetition

apple.com/mac

It was easy to see that the Apple website uses a lot of repition with the use of the 'I' alone but they also have their device types organized according to phones, tablets, and computers. Not only that but the entire layout is consistent throughout the website regardless of what section that you're on.
